Quinnipiac Software Engineering

Frequently Asked Questions

How was the transition from high school to freshman year?

The transition for students from high school to freshman year is an ajustment. Students have more work to get done, but don't have to be in class as many hours as high school. Besides the workload students can meet other software engineers and engineering majors in general through engineering organizations like the Quinnipiac Computing Club (QCC) and also the Quinnipiac University Engineering Student Organization (QUESO).


Do you need coding experience coming into the program?

No programming experience is needed to come into the program. The first two courses teaches students basic coding principles in two different languages. Scala is used to teach the overall basics of programming to students. In the second course students learn Java and learn data structures and more complex things that coding can do.


How helpful are the professors?

Professors in the Software Engineering program are extremely helpful. They can always be reached by email or stopping by during office hours. If they're not busy and in there office not during office hours they are more then happy to help out a student or just chat.


Does the program offer any connection to get any internships or jobs outside of the classroom?

The program offers some connections to get internships or jobs outside of the classroom. The director of the department, Professor Blake, will send out any job or internships he see. The department also has a connection with the Apprentice 100 program which is a great way for students to find internships or even a job. The school also hosts career fairs for students to attend and make connections with companies.
